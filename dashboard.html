Mobility Entropy ?

How predictable your movement patterns are
What is Mobility Entropy?

Measures diversity in your location patterns. Higher = more varied/unpredictable movement. Lower = more routine behavior.

0-1 Highly routine (one dominant location)
1-2 Balanced (2-4 regular locations)
2+ Diverse, unpredictable movement

Example: Visiting 3 places equally = 1.58 bits. Visiting one place 90% of the time = ~0.5 bits.

Dwell Time Patterns temporal

Hierarchical analysis of time spent at locations using thesis-defined dwell categories

How this works

Visits are classified into four hierarchical categories: micro-pauses (<3 min), short stops (3-15 min), medium dwells (15-60 min), and extended stays (>60 min). This framework reveals temporal patterns in how you engage with different locations throughout the day and week.

How this works

Walk Score (0-100) measures walkability based on proximity to amenities. Walking Rate is calculated by grouping GPS points into ~200m grid cells and matching to motion sensor readings. Efficiency Index = (Your Walking %) ÷ (Expected Walking %). All metrics exclude locations within 500m of detected home.

Walking Distance
How this works
Speed Calculation: Distance between consecutive GPS points ÷ time elapsed.
Uses Haversine formula for accurate spherical distance.

Speed Thresholds (research-based):
• Stationary: <0.15 m/s (0.5 km/h)
• Walking: 0.15-2.0 m/s (0.5-7.2 km/h)
• Cycling: 2.0-8.0 m/s (7.2-29 km/h)
• Driving: >8.0 m/s (29+ km/h)

Likely Walking Routes: Consecutive segments where speed stays in walking range for 3+ points with directional movement (not just stationary).
